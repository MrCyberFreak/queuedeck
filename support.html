Reporting a bug? Include your Windows version, the QueueDeck version (Help → About), and what you expected vs. what happened. Screenshots of the queue view help enormously.

Common fixes

“Connect TikTok” opens the browser but nothing happens afterward

Your browser may have blocked the redirect back to the app. Complete the approval on TikTok's page, then return to QueueDeck — if it still shows “waiting,” click Account → Connect TikTok again; the flow is safe to restart.

A scheduled post shows “MISSED”

QueueDeck publishes from your machine, so the computer must be on and online at slot time. Missed posts are held safely; open the post and choose Publish now or move it to the next free slot.

The analytics view is empty

Analytics need a connected account with at least one public video. If your videos are set to private, the platform's data interface returns nothing to connected apps.

I revoked access from TikTok's settings — now what?

That's fully supported: QueueDeck detects the revocation, marks the account disconnected, and stops all publishing. Reconnect anytime from Account → Connect TikTok.

Data removal

QueueDeck stores your drafts, schedule, and analytics history locally on your computer. Uninstalling the app and deleting its data folder (%APPDATA%\QueueDeck) removes everything. Disconnecting your account deletes the stored access tokens. Details in the privacy policy.
