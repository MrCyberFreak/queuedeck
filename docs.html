1. Getting started

QueueDeck is a Windows desktop application. It runs entirely on your machine — there is no QueueDeck account to create and no cloud service holding your content.

  1. Download QueueDeck for Windows (v0.5.0 preview, zip). New builds appear on the releases page.
  2. Unzip it anywhere and run QueueDeck.exe.
  3. Connect your TikTok account (next section) and set your posting slots.
System requirements: Windows 10 or 11, 300 MB free disk space, an internet connection for publishing and analytics refresh.

2. Connecting your TikTok account

QueueDeck uses TikTok's official login (Login Kit). You sign in on TikTok's own page in your browser; QueueDeck never sees or stores your password.

  1. In QueueDeck, open Account → Connect TikTok.
  2. Your browser opens TikTok's authorization page. Review the requested permissions and approve.
  3. QueueDeck receives a revocable access grant and shows your display name and avatar in the top bar.

QueueDeck requests only the permissions it uses:

  • user.info.basic — your display name and avatar, so the app can show which account is connected.
  • user.info.stats — your follower/like counts, shown in the analytics dashboard.
  • video.list — your public videos and their view/like/comment/share counts, for the analytics history.
  • video.upload — send a post to your TikTok inbox as a draft you finish in the TikTok app.
  • video.publish — publish a post you approved directly to your profile at its scheduled time.

3. Composing a post

Open Deck → New post and drop in a video file (MP4/MOV/WebM) or up to 35 photos for a carousel. The composer walks you through every required setting:

  • Caption — editable up to the platform limit, with hashtag support.
  • Privacy — chosen from your account's own available options. There is no default; you pick it for every post.
  • Interactions — allow or disallow comments, Duet, and Stitch. Options your account has disabled are shown disabled here too.
  • AI-generated content — switch this on if the post's content is AI-generated; the platform will label it accordingly.
  • Commercial content — mark a post as promoting yourself or a third party where that applies; QueueDeck applies the platform's required disclosure settings.
  • Cover frame — pick the video frame or photo used as the cover.

Before anything is transmitted, QueueDeck shows a full preview with the platform's Music Usage Confirmation. Nothing uploads until you approve the preview.

4. Slots and the queue

Slots are your standing posting times — for example Tue/Thu 19:00 and Sat 12:00. Approved posts fill the next free slot automatically, or you can pin a post to an exact date and time.

  • Queue view — the week at a glance: what posted, what's next, what's waiting.
  • Reorder — drag posts between slots; pinned posts stay put.
  • Pause the deck — one switch stops all publishing until you resume. Scheduled posts hold their content but will not go out while paused.
  • Draft mode — send a post to your TikTok inbox instead of publishing directly; you finish and publish it in the TikTok app yourself.

After a post publishes, QueueDeck tracks its processing status and links to the live post once the platform confirms it is publicly available.

5. Analytics

The analytics view pulls your video list and per-video view, like, comment, and share counts through TikTok's official data interface, plus your account-level follower and like totals. QueueDeck keeps a local history so you can see trends over time — which slots perform, which formats carry, and how a new post compares to your baseline.

QueueDeck reads only the metrics TikTok makes available to connected apps. Deeper dashboard metrics visible in TikTok Studio (e.g. watch-time breakdowns) are not exposed to any third-party app; QueueDeck will never claim otherwise.

6. Disconnecting and data removal

  • Disconnect in-app: Account → Disconnect removes QueueDeck's access grant and deletes the stored tokens from your machine.
  • Revoke from TikTok: you can also remove QueueDeck from your TikTok account's connected-apps settings at any time; the app's access ends immediately.
  • Local data: drafts, media, schedule, and analytics history are files on your computer. Uninstalling QueueDeck (or deleting its data folder) removes them.

7. FAQ

Why did my scheduled post not publish?

Check three things: the deck isn't paused, your machine was on and online at the slot time, and your account connection is still valid (the top bar shows a warning if access was revoked or expired). A missed slot is retried per your settings and clearly marked in the queue view.

Does QueueDeck bypass any platform limits?

No. QueueDeck respects the platform's posting limits and interfaces. If the platform declines a post, QueueDeck shows the reason and leaves the post safely in your queue.

Can I use QueueDeck on more than one account?

Multi-account support is planned. Early-access builds support one connected account.
